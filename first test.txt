Final Plan: Custom Smart Watch
Core Components
Microcontroller + BLE

nRF52840 (Nordic Semiconductor) – powerful Cortex-M4F with built-in BLE 5.2, plenty of GPIO, SPI, I2C, and ultra-low power sleep modes.
Why: Best-in-class BLE, large community, and enough performance to handle display, touch, and step counting.
Display

1.3" round color TFT (resolution ~240×240) with GC9A01 driver IC (SPI interface).
Driver IC: GC9A01 (already on the display module).
Touch Input (Ring)

Capacitive touch controller: MPR121 (12-channel I2C capacitive touch sensor).
Use: Connect segmented ring electrodes to the MPR121 to detect swipe/tap around the display.
Navigation (Scroll + Press)

Mechanical rotary encoder with built-in push button (e.g., PEC11R series). This provides the physical scroll and click you wanted, separate from the touch ring.
Step Tracking

BMA400 (Bosch) – ultra-low power 3-axis accelerometer with built-in step detection and pedometer.
Alternative: LIS3DH (STMicro) – also low power, but BMA400 is more efficient for continuous step counting.
Timekeeping (RTC)

PCF8563 (NXP) – low power I2C real-time clock with battery backup.
Why: < 0.25 µA in standby, accurate enough for watch use.
Note: Use a separate coin cell (CR2032) to keep time when main battery is dead.
Battery & Power Management

LiPo battery (~200 mAh) – enough for ~7 days with moderate use.
MCP73831 (Microchip) – USB-C LiPo charger IC with charge status.
MCP1700-3.3V (Microchip) – low quiescent current LDO regulator (1.6 µA) to power everything at 3.3V.
USB-C receptacle (with proper CC resistors for 5V charging).
Other

Passive components (resistors, capacitors, pull-ups) for all ICs.
PCB with 4 layers (recommended for mixed-signal and BLE antenna).
Special: you'll need a BLE antenna design (either chip antenna or PCB trace) – consider using a pre-certified nRF52840 module (e.g., Raytac MDBT50Q) to simplify RF.
